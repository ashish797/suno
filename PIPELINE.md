## 🎯 THE BIG PICTURE

We are building a **repeatable, data-driven pipeline** that takes a song from raw concept to viral release — entirely in-house, no label needed.

**The Core Loop:**
```
RESEARCH → ANALYSIS → LYRICS → PROMPTS → GENERATE → ITERATE → RELEASE → TRACK
     ↑                                                                    |
     └────────────────────────── LEARN & REPEAT ──────────────────────────┘
```

**Rules of Engagement:**
- I research, analyze, write, and prompt. You evaluate with human judgment.
- You generate on Suno. You have the final say on what gets released.
- Everything gets documented. Every song teaches us something.
- We move fast, fail cheap, and iterate constantly.

## 🔬 STAGE 1 — RESEARCH

**Goal:** Build a factual foundation. What is actually working in Hindi indie right now?

### 1.1 — Trending Topics & Emotions
- What emotions/themes dominate Hindi indie hits right now?
  - Heartbreak / Moving on
  - Self-love / Independence
  - Modern dating chaos
  - Party / Travel freedom
  - Nostalgia / Past relationships
  - Urban loneliness
  - Family pressure / Society
  - Success flex
- Track emerging topics (what's starting to rise)

### 1.2 — Viral Song Deconstruction
- Identify 15-20 recent Hindi indie songs that went viral (last 6-18 months)
- For each: virality path, hook, emotional core, release context
- Sources: Spotify India charts, JioSaavn trending, YouTube Shorts audio library, Instagram Reels trending audio

### 1.3 — Platform Analysis
Each platform rewards different things:
- **Spotify India:** Playlist inclusion (India Top 50, Today's Top Hits India, etc.), saves, skip rate
- **JioSaavn:** Regional reach, Bollywood crossover potential, radio play
- **YouTube Shorts:** Audio sync rate, clip duration, replay value
- **Instagram Reels:** Share rate, Saves, Stories reposting
- **Apple Music India:** Editorial curation, charts

### 1.4 — Seasonal Calendar
Map out the Indian music calendar:
- **January:** New Year energy, resolution songs
- **February:** Valentine's season (love + heartbreak peaks)
- **March:** Spring / renewal
- **April:** Holi (festival songs, fun/colloquial)
- **May-June:** Summer (travel, freedom, party)
- **July-August:** Monsoon (romance, nostalgia peaks)
- **September:** Back-to-energy, new beginnings
- **October:** Festive season (Dussehra, Durga Puja)
- **November:** Diwali buildup (celebration, family)
- **December:** Year-end, party season, Christmas crossover

### 1.5 — Audience Insights
- Age demographic: 16-22 (Gen Z) vs 22-35 (millennials)
- Urban geography: Metro vs Tier-2/Tier-3 cities
- Language: Pure Hindi vs Hinglish ratio
- Listening contexts: Late night, workout, commute, party, heartbreak at 2am
- What makes them share: Emotional hit, quotable line, relatable story

### 1.6 — Competitive Landscape
- Who else is doing Hindi indie AI-generated music?
- What are they doing well?
- What's missing that we can exploit?

## 🧠 STAGE 2 — ANALYSIS

**Goal:** Reverse-engineer WHY songs work. Build reusable frameworks.

### 2.1 — Structural Patterns
- Song length: Optimal 2:00-3:30 for streaming
- Verse → Chorus ratio
- Hook placement (how early does the hook appear?)
- Pre-chorus buildup patterns
- Outro structure (fade vs full stop vs call-to-action)

### 2.2 — Hook Analysis
- What makes a hook "viral"?
- Syllable count patterns (typically 6-8 syllables per line for Hindi)
- Rhyme scheme types that work in Hindi
- The "one line" phenomenon — which line in a hit song is the most quoted?
- Hook positions that trigger replay behavior

### 2.3 — Lyrical Templates
- The "3-act structure" for Hindi songs
- Opening line patterns (what grabs attention in first 5 seconds)
- Chorus architecture (2-4 lines, repeated, with variation)
- Verse storytelling frameworks (problem → emotion → resolution)
- Bridge formula (the emotional twist)

### 2.4 — Production Signatures
- BPM ranges that dominate (70-90 for emotional, 100-130 for upbeat)
- Common keys in Hindi indie
- Instrumentation trends (acoustic guitar heavy? Piano? Electronic?)
- Drop patterns (if any)
- Vocal production: breathy vs clear, auto-tune level, Hindi pronunciation clarity

### 2.5 — Release Timing Analysis
- Best day: Friday (Spotify/Apple) / Saturday (JioSaavn)
- Best time: 5-7 PM IST (catches evening commute + playlist updates)
- Release frequency: What's the ideal cadence?

### 2.6 — Cross-Song Pattern Analysis
- Do songs with English hooks perform better?
- Do shorter songs (<2:30) get more streams?
- What % of viral songs have a "reel-friendly snippet"?

## ✍️ STAGE 3 — LYRICS (Multi-Stage)

**Goal:** Generate lyrics that are structurally sound, emotionally resonant, and Suno-ready.

### 3.0 — Pre-Lyric Input (from Stage 1 & 2)
- Target topic / emotion
- Target mood
- Target length
- Target BPM
- Platform priority

### 3.1 — Title + Hook Generator
- Generate 10 title options that:
  - Are short (3-6 words)
  - Contain an emotional hook or intriguing phrase
  - Are quotable / screenshot-worthy
  - Have search potential
- Pick top 1-2 to develop

### 3.2 — Chorus Development
- Write 3-4 chorus options (4-8 lines each)
- Focus: The hook must be in the first 2 lines
- Rhyme scheme: AABB or ABAB works well in Hindi
- Emotional arc: Peak, not setup
- Repeatability: Can a non-musician sing it back after one listen?

### 3.3 — Verse 1 (Setup)
- Establish the story / emotion
- Length: 4-8 lines
- Build toward the chorus
- Don't give everything away — leave room for the chorus to land

### 3.4 — Verse 2 (Payoff)
- Continue the story from Verse 1
- Take the emotion deeper or provide resolution
- Bridge the gap to the chorus differently than Verse 1

### 3.5 — Bridge / Alternative Section
- The emotional twist
- Often shorter, different melody feel
- Can be: a spoken word moment, a call-and-response, or a melodic variation
- Not every song needs a bridge, but viral songs often have one

### 3.6 — Outro
- How does the song end?
- Fade out / Full stop / Tag (artist credit mention) / Call to action
- Consider: Does the ending encourage replay?

### 3.7 — Hindi-Phonetic Refinement
- Check for Hindi sound compatibility (some English phrases sound awkward in Suno)
- Romanized Hindi guidance for pronunciation clarity
- Accent markers if needed

### 3.8 — The Hooks Bank
Maintain a living document of reusable hooks:
- Breakup hooks
- Party/freedom hooks
- Self-love hooks
- Nostalgia hooks
- Romance hooks
These get recycled and reworked across songs.

## 🎼 STAGE 4 — SUNO v5.5 PROMPTS

**Goal:** Convert lyrics into Suno-ready prompts that consistently generate usable songs.

### 4.1 — Prompt Anatomy (How a Suno Prompt Works)

A Suno prompt has 5 layers:

```
[STYLE OF MUSIC] + [MOOD/VIBE] + [INSTRUMENTATION] + [VOCAL GUIDANCE] + [STRUCTURE]
```

**Example Structure:**
```
Upbeat Hindi indie pop, male vocal, acoustic guitar and synth,
catchy chorus, 2:30 duration, modern urban feel, heartfelt lyrics,
BPM 110, C major, party anthemen vibe
```

### 4.2 — Style Layer
- Primary genre reference
- Fusion elements (indie + pop? Rock? Electronic?)
- Regional flavor (Delhi indie? Mumbai vibe? Bengali influence?)
- Reference artists (without copying): "like early 2000s Hindi indie"

### 4.3 — Mood/Vibe Layer
- One-word mood + elaboration
- Energy level (high/medium/low)
- Emotional target (euphoric, melancholic, defiant, dreamy)

### 4.4 — Instrumentation Layer
- Primary instrument(s)
- Production texture (raw, polished, lo-fi, cinematic)
- What NOT to include (negative prompting)

### 4.5 — Vocal Guidance
- Male / Female / Duet
- Tone: warm, gritty, smooth, breathy, powerful
- Hindi pronunciation clarity (important for Suno)
- Emotional delivery style
- Vocal register (head voice vs chest voice feel)

### 4.6 — Structural Guidance
- Desired length
- Where the hook should hit
- Chorus repeat structure
- Any specific section notes

### 4.7 — Prompt Templates (Proven Starting Points)
- Build 5-10 templates for different song types
- Templates for: Heartbreak, Party, Self-Love, Travel, Nostalgia, Modern Romance

### 4.8 — Negative Prompting
What to tell Suno to AVOID:
- Auto-tune overuse
- Bollywood clichés
- Generic pop sound
- English-only lyrics when Hindi is requested
- Awkward Hindi pronunciation
- Repetitive / boring instrumental

### 4.9 — Prompt Versioning
Every song prompt gets versioned:
- `prompt-v1.md` — Initial prompt
- `prompt-v2.md` — Refined after first generate
- `prompt-final.md` — The one that worked

## 🎵 STAGE 5 — GENERATE & ITERATE

**Goal:** Use Suno v5.5 to generate songs, evaluate, and refine until we have a keeper.

### 5.1 — Suno v5.5 Settings
- Model version: v5.5 (latest)
- Generation mode: Full song vs Custom (we use Custom for control)
- Duration: 2:00-3:30 (set explicitly)
- Quality: Highest available
- Lyrics input: The multi-stage lyrics from Stage 3
- Style prompt: From Stage 4

### 5.2 — Generation Workflow
1. Run initial prompt with full lyrics + style guide
2. Evaluate: Does the melody match the emotional intent?
3. Check: Is the Hindi pronunciation acceptable?
4. Check: Is the hook landing where expected?
5. Iterate prompt or lyrics, generate again
6. Target: 3-5 generations per song before deciding

### 5.3 — Evaluation Criteria
Score each generation on:
- **Hook Strength** (1-10): Does the chorus hit?
- **Melody Quality** (1-10): Is it memorable?
- **Production** (1-10): Does it sound modern?
- **Vocal** (1-10): Hindi clarity, emotional delivery
- **Overall Vibe** (1-10): Would you listen again?

### 5.4 — A/B Testing
- Generate 2-3 prompt variations simultaneously
- Compare outputs
- Keep the best elements from each

### 5.5 — The "Good Enough" Threshold
Not every song needs to be perfect. Shipping > Perfect.
- If 3+ criteria score 7/10, it's release-ready
- If something fundamental is broken (hook missing, lyrics garbled), regenerate

### 5.6 — Generation Log
Every attempt logged with:
- Date/time
- Prompt used
- Scores
- Notes (what to change next time)

## 🚀 STAGE 6 — RELEASE

**Goal:** Get the song into the world with maximum impact.

### 6.1 — Cover Art
- Suno generates cover art — evaluate + regenerate if needed
- Alternative: Use AI image tools for custom art
- Design principles:
  - Bold, simple, high-contrast
  - Song title legible at small sizes
  - Emotional mood matches the song
  - No clutter

### 6.2 — Metadata Optimization
- **Title:** Clear, searchable, has the hook word
- **Artist Name:** Consistent across releases
- **Genre Tags:** Hindi, Indie, Pop, etc.
- **Language:** Hindi (primary)
- **Description:** Include keywords naturally
- **Explicit tag:** Based on content

### 6.3 — Release Timing
- **Day:** Friday (catches international + Indian playlist updates)
- **Time:** 5:00-7:00 PM IST
- **Advance upload:** Tuesday-Wednesday (to clear any content ID issues)
- **Set availability:** Global immediately

### 6.4 — Platform-Specific Upload
1. **Spotify for Artists:** Upload 48h early if possible, claim artist profile
2. **JioSaavn:** Set artist profile, verify
3. **YouTube Music:** Sync with YouTube channel
4. **Apple Music:** Connect via App Store Connect

### 6.5 — Playlist Pitching
- Pitch to Spotify editorial: 7-14 days before release via Spotify for Artists
- JioSaavn editorial pitch
- YouTube Music editorial
- Key playlists to target:
  - India's Indie Scene
  - Hindi Hits
  - Mood-based playlists (Late Night, Heartbreak, etc.)

### 6.6 — Social Launch Strategy
**Pre-Release (1 week before):**
- Teaser clips (15-30 seconds) on Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts
- Behind-the-scenes (you in Suno, working on it)

**Release Day:**
- Full upload
- Post the Reel/Short with the song's best 15 seconds
- Caption with the quotable hook line
- Add to bio link: recommend streaming platform

**Post-Release (Week 1):**
- Respond to every comment
- Post 2-3 additional clips (different sections of the song)
- Engage in comments on other accounts using the audio

### 6.7 — Hashtag Strategy
- Instagram: #hindisong #indiemusic #hindiindie #newmusic #sunoai #audiotrack
- YouTube: Let the algorithm handle, focus on title optimization
- Don't over-tag (3-5 max per post)

### 6.8 — Release Checklist
```
[ ] Song exported from Suno
[ ] Cover art approved
[ ] Metadata filled (title, artist, genre, language)
[ ] Pitched to Spotify editorial
[ ] Uploaded to all platforms
[ ] Release date/time confirmed
[ ] Pre-release teaser posted
[ ] Release day social post drafted
[ ] Streaming links collected (for bio link tool)
```

## 📊 STAGE 7 — TRACK & LEARN

**Goal:** Measure, learn, and feed insights back into the pipeline.

### 7.1 — Metrics to Track
Per song, weekly for the first month:
- **Spotify:** Streams, Saves, Skip Rate, Playlist Adds, Followers
- **JioSaavn:** Plays, Favirites, Shares
- **YouTube/Shorts:** Views on any Shorts using the audio, Watch time
- **Instagram:** Reels using audio, Views, Shares, Saves
- **Overall:** Total impressions, Total streams

### 7.2 — Success Benchmarks
- **Week 1:** 1,000+ streams = Green flag
- **Week 4:** 10,000+ streams = On track
- **Going viral:** 100,000+ streams in 30 days

### 7.3 — Failure Analysis
For songs that underperform:
- Which metric failed? (streams? saves? shares?)
- Was it the hook? The production? The topic?
- Was it release timing?
- Was it promotion?

### 7.4 — Learnings Document
After each song, log:
- What worked
- What didn't
- What to try differently next time

### 7.5 — Pattern Recognition
Over time, answer:
- Which topics/hooks perform best?
- What's the ideal song length?
- Which production styles get more saves?
- Is there a pattern in release timing?

## 🔄 PIPELINE CONSTRAINTS & RULES

1. **Speed over perfection** — Ship fast, learn faster
2. **Document everything** — If it's not logged, it didn't happen
3. **Human decision point** — Every major choice has you in the loop
4. **No ego** — Data over intuition. If it's not working, change it.
5. **Consistency beats one-hit wonder** — We build a catalog, not a single viral moment
6. **Learn in public** — Documenting is how we refine
