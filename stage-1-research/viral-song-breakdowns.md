**Virality path:** Bollywood film → Instagram Reels dance challenge → Audio library → Streaming
**Key lesson:** A single iconic visual moment + a repeatable sound = viral compound interest.

## 📊 WHAT THE DATA TELLS US

### What makes a Hindi indie song go viral on Reels/Shorts:

1. **One-line quotable hook** — First or second line of chorus. Must be screenshot-worthy.
2. **Length:** Under 3 minutes is ideal. Under 2:30 for maximum Reels use.
3. **Production:** Clean but not overproduced. Lo-fi indie is a consistent winner.
4. **Vocal:** Warm, slightly breathy. NOT overly polished Auto-Tune.
5. **Emotional trigger:** Either deeply relatable (heartbreak) OR aspirational (party/freedom).
6. **Memorable melody:** If you can hum it after one listen, it has legs.
7. **Hindi pronunciation clarity:** If Hindi listeners can't understand the words, they won't share.

## 🧩 VIRAL SONG STRUCTURES

### Most common successful structure:
```
[Intro - 5 sec - sets mood]
[Verse 1 - establishes story] → 8-12 lines
[Chorus - HOOK lands here] → 4 lines, repeated
[Verse 2 - deepens story] → 8 lines
[Chorus x2] → anchor the hook
[Bridge - emotional twist] → 4 lines
[Chorus final] → fade or full stop
[Outro - 5-10 sec] → tag or silence
```

### The "hook placement" rule:
**Hook MUST land within the first 40 seconds.** If the hook takes too long to arrive, Reels users scroll away. Modern listeners have zero patience.

## 💡 KEY INSIGHT: THE "REEL CLIP" TEST

Before finalizing any song, ask: **"Does this song have a 15-second clip that someone would use on Reels?"**

If the answer is no, the song will struggle on social even if it's great musically.

The song needs:
- A clean start point (no long intro)
- A self-contained hook (doesn't need full song context)
- Visual/emotional stand-alone appeal
