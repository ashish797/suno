# 👥 STAGE 1 — AUDIENCE INSIGHTS

*Who we're making music for, how they listen, and why they share.*

---

## 🎯 PRIMARY AUDIENCE: THE CORE LISTENER

**Age:** 16-28 (Gen Z dominant on Reels, indie discovery)
**Secondary:** 28-40 (millennials, Spotify/Apple Music, playlist curators)
**Geography:** Metro + Tier-2 cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Pune, Hyderabad, Chennai, Lucknow, Jaipur)

### The Two Audience Modes:
1. **Discovery Mode:** Young audience finds songs via Reels/Shorts → goes to streaming
2. **Active Listening Mode:** Older audience seeks out playlists → discovers via editorial

---

## 🎧 HOW THEY LISTEN

### Contexts (when songs get played):
- **Late night (10 PM - 3 AM):** Emotional songs, heartbreak, introspection. BIGGEST streaming window for indie.
- **Morning commute (8-10 AM):** Upbeat, motivational
- **Evening commute (5-7 PM):** Wind-down, mood music
- **Work/study (10 AM-5 PM):** Lo-fi, background
- **Workout:** High BPM, party anthems
- **Party/social:** Upbeat, crowd-pleasers

### Key insight:
**Late-night emotional songs get the most streams per listener.** One heartbreak song at 2 AM generates more replay value than a party song at a gathering.

---

## 📱 HOW THEY DISCOVER

### Top discovery paths (2025-2026):
1. **Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts** ← #1 entry point for viral songs
2. **Friend recommendations** (DM'd links)
3. **Spotify algorithmic** (Release Radar, Discover Weekly)
4. **Spotify editorial playlists**
5. **JioSaavn home screen**
6. **YouTube music recommendations**

### The cross-platform discovery loop:
```
Shorts/Reels viral audio → 
Search on Spotify → 
Add to library → 
Spotify algorithm fires →
Recommendations to others →
YouTube Music picks up →
Cycle repeats
```

## 💬 WHY THEY SHARE

The #1 question to ask: **"Would someone send this to a specific person?"**

### Top share triggers:
1. **"That's exactly how I feel"** — Emotional accuracy
2. **"This is SO us"** — Duo/couple songs
3. **"My ex needs to hear this"** — Ex-directed messages
4. **"This is my vibe"** — Identity expression
5. **"Forwarding to my best friend at 2 AM"** — 2 AM emotional resonance

### The screenshot test:
If a lyric would make someone screenshot it and send it as a message, it's a viral lyric. These lines show up on:
- Instagram Stories
- WhatsApp forwards
- Bio link tool shares

---

## 🎭 WHAT MAKES THEM RETURN (SAVE VS STREAM)

### Saves vs. Streams:
- **Streams:** Passive listening, one-time discovery
- **Saves:** "I want to hear this again and again" = the REAL engagement signal

### Songs that get saves:
1. Emotional accuracy (exactly the right feeling)
2. Personal attachment ("this is MY song now")
3. Nostalgia trigger ("takes me back to...")
4. Functional ("perfect for late night" playlist material)

### Songs that get streams but not saves:
- Too generic
- Hook fades after first listen
- No emotional depth beyond first impression

## 🌍 REGIONAL / LANGUAGE PATTERNS

### Pure Hindi:
- Most universal reach
- Works across North, Central, East India
- Slight disadvantage in South India (unless bilingual)

### Hinglish (Hindi + English mix):
- Best of both worlds
- English hook + Hindi verse = common successful formula
- "English chorus, Hindi verses" lets you get the best melodic phrasing

### Caution:
- Too much English kills the "indie Hindi" authenticity
- Too much pure Hindi can limit viral ceiling outside Hindi belt
- **Sweet spot: 70-80% Hindi, 20-30% English in key hook moments**

---

## 📊 DEMOGRAPHIC BREAKDOWN

### Gen Z (16-22):
- Found primarily via Reels/Shorts
- Share FIRST, think later
- More likely to use audio in their own content
- High tolerance for lo-fi/raw production
- Very emotionally expressive

### Millennials (23-35):
- Found via Spotify editorial + word of mouth
- More selective about what they save
- Higher standards for production quality
- More likely to buy/Subscribe
- Driver of "prestige" indie

### Tier-2/Tier-3 cities:
- JioSaavn dominant platform
- Bollywood-crossover resonance (more accepting of production value)
- Strong on local language emotional resonance
- Less picky about "indie authenticity"

---

## 🎯 THE "2 AM TEST"

This is the ultimate filter for a Hindi indie song:

> *"Would someone lie awake at 2 AM, have this song on repeat, and feel understood?"*

If yes → strong save candidate
If no → it's a stream-at-a-party song, not a life-soundtrack song

Life-soundtrack songs build careers. Party songs make viral moments.
