# 🪝 STAGE 2 — HOOK ANALYSIS

*The single most important element in a viral song. Everything else is setup for this.*

---

## 🎯 WHAT IS A HOOK?

The hook is the part of the song that:
1. **Stops the scroll** (on Reels/Shorts)
2. **Makes you listen again** (on streaming)
3. **Gets texted to someone** (organic sharing)
4. **Stays in your head after the song ends** (the earworm)

The hook can be:
- A melodic line (the most common)
- A lyrical line (the quotable one-liner)
- A combination of both

**For our pipeline:** We focus on **melodic + lyrical hooks** that land together. The line that stops the scroll is also the line that gets stuck in your head.

---

## 🧠 THE HOOK-FIRST MINDSET

### The hook-first songwriting approach:

1. **Start with the hook.** Before writing anything else, find the hook. One line that captures the entire emotional thesis of the song.

2. **Build the song around it.** Verse 1 sets up the hook. Verse 2 supports the hook. Bridge provides contrast. Every section exists to make the hook land harder.

3. **The hook must be in the first 40 seconds.** If it's buried at 90 seconds, the song is fighting the algorithm instead of using it.

### The hook thesis formula:

**Ask: What is the ONE thing I want the listener to feel or remember?**
That answer IS the hook.

- "Moving on feels impossible but I'm doing it anyway" → hook is the moment of choosing to leave
- "I don't need you, I never did" → hook is the declaration of independence
- "It's 2 AM and I can't stop thinking about what we were" → hook is the timestamp + ache

## ✍️ THE ANATOMY OF A VIRAL HOOK LINE

### The 6-8 syllable rule:

**For Hindi, the sweet spot is 6-8 syllables per hook line.**

Why: Hindi has a natural rhythm in short phrases. Too long and the melody feels clunky. Too short and it doesn't carry enough weight.

### Hook line structures that work in Hindi:

**Structure 1: Two-part revelation**
```
[Part A — sets up] + [Part B — the twist]
Tera saath tha, dil nahi tha (8 syllables)
Ab jaana hai, wapas nahi (8 syllables)
```
✅ Most common. First half sets expectation, second half subverts.

**Structure 2: Sensory image + emotion**
```
[Image/Detail] + [Feeling]
2 AM ki raat, akeli raat (8 syllables)
Neon ke neeche, khawab toot gaye (8 syllables)
```
✅ Works well for lo-fi/late-night songs. Visual + emotional.

**Structure 3: Simple declaration**
```
[Subject] + [State/Action]
Main apni pehli priority ban gaya (9 syllables — slightly long but works)
Tera intezaar, main nahi karta (8 syllables)
```
✅ Works for self-worth anthems.

**Structure 4: The question**
```
[Question that implies answer]
Tu kahan hai, dil kahan hai? (8 syllables)
Kyun aisa hota hai? (5 syllables — too short alone, needs context)
```
✅ Powerful. The implied answer hits harder than saying it.

## 📍 HOOK PLACEMENT

### Where the hook appears:

**Option A: End of Verse 1 → Chorus (most common)**
```
Verse 1 line 8 sets up the hook →
Chorus line 1 IS the hook
```
✅ Traditional, reliable

**Option B: Starts the song (chorus-first)**
```
Chorus plays first (hook lands immediately) →
Then Verse 1 provides context
```
✅ Maximum hook impact. Used for high-intensity songs.

**Option C: Pre-chorus buildup → hook in chorus**
```
Pre-chorus (2-4 lines) builds tension →
Chorus line 1 is the hook release
```
✅ Best for emotional escalation songs.

### The hook must repeat:
A hook that appears once is not a hook — it's just a good line.
**Minimum: 2 full repetitions of the chorus.**
**Better: 3 repetitions (with slight variation on the 3rd).**

## 🎤 THE MELODIC HOOK

### What makes a melody hooky?

**1. Contour: The shape of the note sequence**

The melody goes UP toward the hook, or it hits a HIGH note and stays there.
Downward melodies feel like endings. Hooks need to feel like something landed.

**Pattern that works:**
```
Verse melody: stays in middle register
Pre-chorus: rises toward the top
Hook: peaks at the highest note, then resolves
```

**2. Repetition with variation**

The hook melody repeats exactly, but something around it changes:
- First chorus: just the hook
- Second chorus: hook + backing vocals
- Third chorus: hook + fuller production

**3. Syllable-to-note ratio**

In Hindi:
- 6-8 syllables per line
- Each syllable gets roughly 1 note (not always, but roughly)
- Hook lines that land on one or two notes feel strongest

**4. The "hummable" test**

If you can hum the hook line without the words, and someone recognizes the song — the melody is doing its job.

## 🔄 HOOK VARIATION TECHNIQUES

### Variation 1: Final chorus extension
```
Chorus 1: 4 lines
Chorus 2: 4 lines
Chorus 3: 6 lines (adds 2 bridge/transition lines before or after)
```
✅ Adds finality without changing the core hook.

### Variation 2: Chorus lyric swap
```
Chorus 1: "Ab jaana hai, wapas nahi"
Chorus 2: "Phir jaana hai, wapas nahi"
```
✅ Slight variation maintains freshness while reinforcing the hook.

### Variation 3: Ad-lib / breath before final chorus
```
[Before Chorus 3]
*breath* ... (small musical moment) →
Chorus 3 hits
```
✅ Resets attention before the final landing.

## 🚫 HOOK MISTAKES

### 1. Hook is too long
Lines over 12 syllables struggle to land as hooks. They become verses.

### 2. Hook has no twist
"Main tera hoon" (I am yours) is fine. "Tera hoon, lekin dil nahi" (I am yours but my heart isn't) is a hook.
The second half must subvert the first half.

### 3. Hook requires context to land
If the hook only makes sense after verse 2, it's not a hook — it's a payoff.
A hook should be self-evident in its emotional meaning the first time it plays.

### 4. Hook is buried under production
The vocal must be the clearest element when the hook plays.
If there's a loud synth or drum hit on the hook line, the hook is fighting the mix.

### 5. No repetition
First hook lands, song moves on, listener forgets.
The hook must repeat to embed. This is non-negotiable.

## 📊 HOOK SCORECARD

Rate each potential hook on:
| Criteria | Score (1-5) |
|----------|-------------|
| **Emotion:** Does it hit immediately? | /5 |
| **Brevity:** Is it 6-8 syllables? | /5 |
| **Twist:** Is there an unexpected turn? | /5 |
| **Repeatability:** Would you say it out loud? | /5 |
| **Stand-alone:** Does it make sense without the verse? | /5 |
| **Humability:** Can you hum it? | /5 |

**Total: /30**

**20+ = strong hook, use it**
**15-20 = workable but needs refinement**
**Below 15 = scrap and try again**
