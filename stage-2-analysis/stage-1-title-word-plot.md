## PART B: MAIN WORD ANALYSIS

### B1. Semantic Clusters of Title Words

**CLUSTER 1 — Places/Locations (Physical Anchors):**
- Aasmaan (sky) → emotional distance
- Raasta (path) → life journey
- Bargad (banyan tree) → a meeting place
- Sheher (city) → urban loneliness

**CLUSTER 2 — Time/Finiteness:**
- Kuch din (few days) → impermanence
- Raatein (nights) → memory, intimacy
- Lamha (moment) → desperation, intensity
- Kab tak (how long) → waiting pain

**CLUSTER 3 — Abstract Emotions:**
- Husn (beauty) → surface vs depth
- Kasoor (sin) → guilt, blame
- Nidarr (patience) → endurance
- Unstoppable → empowerment

**CLUSTER 4 — Devotion/Possession:**
- Tum mere ho (you are mine) → ownership
- Tere hi hum (only yours) → belonging
- Dua (prayer) → devotion

**CLUSTER 5 — Questions/Search:**
- Kahan (where) → searching
- Kya (what) → confusion
- "To kya?" (so what) → defiance

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR SUNO SONGWRITING

1. **Title = Hook**: 60% of viral songs use the title AS the hook — make the title word singable and repetition-worthy on its own
2. **Short titles work**: Most are 2-4 syllables. "Gul" (2), "Husn" (2), "Khat" (2), "Sage" (2) — minimalist
3. **Question titles are strong**: "Tu kahan?", "Kho gaye hum kahan?" — they pull the listener in
4. **Possessive/devotional phrases**: "Tere hi hum," "Jo tum mere ho" — claiming the other person
5. **Heartbreak dominates**: 40% of songs are post-breakup. The other big categories: longing (30%), devotion (15%), self-motivation (10%), moment-seizing (5%)
6. **The "Kahan" pattern**: Two of the biggest songs use "kahan" (where) in the title — searching/longing is a massive pattern
7. **Time-sensitive words**: "Kuch din" (few days), "Lamha" (moment), "Raatein" (nights) — time creates urgency
8. **Concrete imagery**: Tree, flower, path, city, bed — abstract emotions grounded in physical places/objects
