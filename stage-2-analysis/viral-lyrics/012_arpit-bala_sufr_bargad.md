## HOOK ANALYSIS

**Hook line:** "Bargad tale intezaar tera main karoon kab tak?"

**Translation:** "How long should I wait for you beneath the banyan tree?"

**Syllable count:** 10 syllables
**Structure:** Question — longing, waiting, devotion tied to a physical place

**What makes it work:**
- "Bargad" (Banyan tree) — iconic Indian imagery, a place where people meet, gossip, fall in love
- "Aashiq baby" (lover) — casual modern addition that grounds it in today's indie sound
- The question "kab tak?" (how long?) is a universal wait-pain question
- "Ek jhalak se pagal" (mad from one glance) — classic but fresh
- Multi-artist collaboration adds dimension

---

## STRUCTURAL PATTERN

- **Hook lands:** First chorus, ~30 seconds
- **Title-driven:** "Bargad" is the anchor word
- **Metaphor:** Tree as meeting place, devotion
- **Modern + traditional mix:** "Aashiq baby" sits alongside "Bargad tale"
- **Runtime:** ~3:00-3:30
