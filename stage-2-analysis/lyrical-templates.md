# 📝 STAGE 2 — LYRICAL TEMPLATES

*Reusable structural frameworks for writing Hindi indie lyrics. Copy, fill in, twist.*

---

## 🎯 HOW TO USE THESE TEMPLATES

These are structural scaffolds. They give you the framework — you bring the specific story, emotion, and twist.

Each template has:
1. **When it works** — which song types/emotions it fits
2. **Why it works** — the emotional logic
3. **The scaffold** — the verse/chorus/bridge structure
4. **What makes it land** — the key principle

---

## 💔 TEMPLATE 1: THE HEARTBREAK ARC

**When it works:** Classic breakup, moving on, "I thought we were something"
**Emotional logic:** Setup → Emotional hit → Reflection → Acceptance
**Anchors:** The hook is the moment of choosing to leave

### Structure:
```
[VERSE 1 — Setup]
Scene-setting: Where, when, what happened
Introduce "you" — what you meant, what went wrong
Lines 1-2: Time/place/emotional state
Lines 3-4: What happened
Lines 5-6: What it meant
Lines 7-8: Bridge to chorus (the hook setups here)

[CHORUS — The Declaration]
Lines 1: THE HOOK — the quotable "I'm done" moment
Lines 2-3: Support/elaboration
Line 4: Finality or thread

[VERSE 2 — Deepening]
Acknowledge the chorus hit
Go deeper — the specific memory, the ongoing ache
Escalate — what it costs to stay gone

[C HORUS x2]

[BRIDGE — Inward Turn]
The narrator turns to themselves
No more "you" — only "me" and "I"
The real truth comes out

[CHORUS x2 / FADE]
```

### Example (fleshed out):
```
[Verse 1]
Raat ko 2 baje phone dekhta hun
Tera naam aata hai, phir sochta hun
Itne din ho gaye, kuch nahi badla
Bas dil ka hissa raha, waqt nahi badla

[Chorus]
Tera saath tha, dil nahi tha
Ab jaana hai, wapas nahi
Pehle tera intezaar tha
Ab meri raah apni hai
```

## 👑 TEMPLATE 2: THE SELF-WORTH DECLARATION

**When it works:** Post-breakup confidence, independence anthem, "I chose myself"
**Emotional logic:** Declaration → Context → Escalation → Final declaration
**Anchors:** The hook is a powerful "I am" statement

### Structure:
```
[VERSE 1 — The Backstory]
What was (the relationship/situation)
What happened (the breaking point)
What I realized (the shift)

[CHORUS — The Declaration]
Line 1: THE HOOK — "I am / I don't need / I chose"
Lines 2-3: The supporting truth
Line 4: The anthem line (shareable, quotable)

[VERSE 2 — The Growth]
The after — how life feels different
What I learned / who I became
No bitterness toward "you" — just strength

[CHORUS x2]

[BRIDGE — The Deeper Truth]
Not about you anymore — about me
The unexpected softness (strength can be gentle)

[CHORUS x2 / FADE]
```

### Example:
```
[Verse 1]
Woh DIN wo gaye jab main rota tha
Ab aaine mein dekhta hun, muskurata hun
Koi attitude nahi, bas sach yeh hai
Pehle tera tha, ab mera sab kuch meri hai

[Chorus]
Main apni pehli priority ban gaya
Pehle khud se mil, phir tera intezaar
Tera intezaar, ab karna chhod diya
Apni raah pe chal raha hun, seedha
```

## 💬 TEMPLATE 3: THE SITUATIONSHIP SONG

**When it works:** No-label relationship, "what are we" exhaustion, undefined emotional investment
**Emotional logic:** Setup → Confusion → Exhaustion → Decision
**Anchors:** The hook is the "define this or I'm out" moment

### Structure:
```
[VERSE 1 — The Setup]
How it started / what it looked like
The ambiguity (good but undefined)
The narrator's hope

[CHORUS — The Hook]
The question (what are you to me?)
The exhaustion (I'm tired of guessing)
The line drawn (or not drawn)

[VERSE 2 — The Reality]
What it actually is (casual, distant, inconsistent)
What the narrator wants (more, or clarity)
The inevitable

[C HORUS x2]

[BRIDGE — The Decision]
After all the back-and-forth
Either: "I'm done waiting" (exit)
Or: "Fine, I'll take what's there" (acceptance)

[C HORUS x2 / FADE]
```

### Example:
```
[Verse 1]
Tere messages aate hain raat ko
Phir subah tak, koi reply nahi
Baat bhi karte hain kabhi-kabhi
Par define karne se always door nahi

[Chorus]
Tu kya hai mere liye? 
Dosti ya pyaar, kuch nahi clear
Itne din se sochta hun
Tera answer aana chahiye
```

## 🌃 TEMPLATE 4: THE LATE-NIGHT / CITY SONG

**When it works:** Urban loneliness, 2 AM introspection, "the city doesn't sleep but I can't"
**Emotional logic:** Scene → The ache → The specific detail → The acceptance
**Anchors:** The hook is the timestamp + emotional state

### Structure:
```
[VERSE 1 — The Scene]
The time (2 AM, 3 AM)
The place (city, window, bed, tea)
The feeling (ache, emptiness, overthinking)

[CHORUS — THE HOOK]
Time stamp + feeling in one line
The specific detail (what triggers the feeling)
The contradiction (city is awake, I'm not)

[VERSE 2 — The Spiral]
What I'm thinking about (you, the past, the future)
What I'm doing about it (nothing, just lying here)
The 2 AM clarity that fades by morning

[C HORUS x2]

[BRIDGE — The Admission]
Not about you anymore, it's about me
The thing I haven't said to myself

[C HORUS x2 / FADE]
```

### Example:
```
[Verse 1]
2 baje ki city soja hai
Par neend nahi aayi, koi taareeka nahi
Khidki ke saamne baitha hun
Chai thandi ho gayi, socha bahut kuch

[Chorus]
2 AM ki raat mein sab kuch visible hai
Jo din mein chhupa, raat mein dikhta hai
Neon ke neeche khawab toot gaye
Par subah tak, sab theek ho gaya
```

## 🎉 TEMPLATE 5: THE PARTY / FREEDOM SONG

**When it works:** Post-breakup freedom, travel, "treat yourself" energy
**Emotional logic:** The invitation → The escape → The freedom → The anthem
**Anchors:** The hook is the "let's go" moment. Fast, energetic, quotable.

### Structure:
```
[INTRO — The vibe (instrumental, 5 sec)]

[VERSE 1 — The Setup]
Where we're starting from (usually something being left behind)
The invitation (let's go / let's forget)

[CHORUS — The Hook]
Short, punchy, repeated
The one-line invitation or declaration
Everything else supports it

[VERSE 2 — The Release]
Where we're going (literally or metaphorically)
What it feels like (freedom, lightness)
The contrast from verse 1

[C HORUS x2]

[BRIDGE — The "why"]
Not just party — why this matters
What we're leaving behind and why it's ok
The one-liner that makes it meaningful

[C HORUS x3 / FULL STOP]
```

## 🎨 TEMPLATE 6: THE MONSOON / RAIN ROMANCE

**When it works:** Rain season, love that intensifies in the rain, nostalgic love
**Emotional logic:** Setup → The rain → The feeling → The longing
**Anchors:** The hook is the sensory moment — rain + love + ache

### Structure:
```
[VERSE 1 — The Setup]
The season / the rain starting
The memory that comes with rain
The person associated with this feeling

[C HORUS — The Hook]
Rain + emotion in the same breath
The specific sensory moment
What it does to me

[VERSE 2 — The Absence]
Rain without you
The thing that changed
Wished you were here

[C HORUS x2]

[BRIDGE — The Nostalgia]
The last time we had rain together
How it felt vs how it feels now
The ache of "almost"

[C HORUS x2 / FADE]
```

## 🧩 TEMPLATE 7: THE "3-ACT" STRUCTURE

For songs that tell a mini-story across 3 acts:

```
[ACT 1 — Beginning]
Who we are, what we have
The setup

[ACT 2 — Middle]
What happened, what went wrong
The conflict

[ACT 3 — End]
Resolution (leaving, accepting, growing)
The takeaway
```

Each act can be 4-8 lines. Works for narrative-driven songs.

## ⚙️ THE RHYME SCHEME GUIDE

### What works in Hindi Indie:

**AABB (couplet rhyme):** Most natural in Hindi. Each two lines rhyme.
```
Tera saath tha, dil nahi tha (A)
Ab jaana hai, wapas nahi (A)
```
✅ Most common, works perfectly

**ABAB:** Every other line rhymes.
```
Tera saath tha (A)
Dil nahi tha (B)
Ab jaana hai (A)
Wapas nahi (B)
```
✅ Works for more complex emotions

**Free verse (no rhyme):** Works for very intimate lo-fi songs.
✅ Only if melody carries it strongly

## ✍️ THE LINE-BY-LINE WRITING RULES

1. **Every line earns its place** — no filler
2. **Sensory details > abstract statements** — "2 AM phone" beats "I miss you"
3. **Surprise in the second half** — first half sets up, second half twists
4. **Active verbs** — "dil toota" > "dil dukha"
5. **Specific nouns** — "chai," "phone," "khidki" > "things," "stuff"
6. **Feelings through actions** — show, don't tell
