### A2. Syllable Distribution
| Syllables | Songs | % |
|-----------|-------|---|
| 3-4 | 4 | 20% |
| 5-6 | 2 | 10% |
| 7-8 | 7 | 35% |
| 9-11 | 6 | 30% |
| 14+ | 1 | 5% |

**Finding:** The "sweet spot" is **7-8 syllables** (35% of songs). But 5-6 syllable hooks also work well (10+35=45% in the 5-8 range). The viral short hooks from our first song attempt (8 syllables for "Baarish Se Pehle") were actually too long — the sweet spot is closer to **4-7 syllables**.

### A3. Short Hooks vs Long Hooks
**Short hooks (4-6 syllables) — INSTANT CATCHY:**
- "खो गए हम कहाँ" (5) — KGHK, 105M+ views
- "क्या कसूर है मेरा" (6) — KASOOR
- "जो तुम मेरे हो" (5) — JTMH, 105M+ views
- "Unstoppable" (4) — word repeated ×8
- "तू है कहाँ?" (4) — TU HAI KAHAN, extremely fast landing
- "Nidarr nidarr nidarr" (3+3+3)

**Medium hooks (7-10 syllables) — FULL CHORUS UNIT:**
- "तेरे बिन जी नहीं पाया" (7)
- "तेरे ही हम हैं / क्या ये प्यार कम है" (8)
- "क़तरा क़तरा पी लूँ तुझे" (8)

**Long hooks (11+ syllables) — NARRATIVE-STYLE:**
- "बरगद के तले इंतज़ार तेरा मैं करूँ कब तक?" (11)
- "अलग आसमान भी है तो क्या? यह दिल ना माने" (10)

**Critical finding for Suno:** Shorter is better for hook memorability. 4-6 syllable hooks work because they're INSTANTLY repeatable. Full-chorus hooks (7-10 syllables) work when the chorus is designed as one flowing unit. **Avoid 11+ syllable hooks for the core tagline** — these belong in narrative-style songs only.

### C3. The "Tere Bin" Pattern
This phrase appears in multiple songs and seems to be a viral pattern:
- "तेरे बिन जी नहीं पाया" (GUL)
- "तेरे बिना अधूरा सा / जी भी नहीं लगता" (TINKA — devanagari)

**The "tere bin" (without you) construction:**
- Creates the core emotional problem: absence of the other person
- Works as a standalone statement
- Can end with "kya?" (what happens without you?) or "nahi paya" (can't live)
- For Suno: "Tere bin" is a proven anchor phrase

### C4. The "Kya" Question Pattern
Songs starting hook with a question:
| Hook | Effect |
|------|--------|
| "क्या कसूर है मेरा" | Blame/injustice question |
| "क्या ये प्यार कम है" | Defensive question |
| "तू है कहाँ?" | Searching question |
| "अलग आसमान भी है तो क्या?" | Defiant question ("so what if") |
| "कब तक?" | Waiting urgency |
| "कैसे सोए जाना?" | Intimate demand |

The "Kya..." question opening is very common — it creates listener engagement because the brain wants the answer.

## PART G: SONGWRITING PATTERNS FOR SUNO

### G1. Hook Writing Rules
1. **Target 5-7 syllables for tagline** — can be embedded in a 2-line (10-14 syllable) couplet
2. **AA rhyme scheme** — two lines that rhyme with each other
3. **Include "Tere bin"** — proven absence/longing anchor
4. **OR include "Kya" question** — creates listener engagement
5. **Use body part or natural image** — grounds the abstract emotion
6. **Keep verb at end** — Hindi verb-final structure creates closure

### G2. What NOT To Do
- ❌ 11+ syllable hooks for tagline portion (they work as full-chorus narrative only)
- ❌ Pure English hooks (only works for UNSTOPPABLE-type motivation songs)
- ❌ ABAB complex rhyme schemes (too forced, loses singability)
- ❌ More than 4 lines in the hook block (gets unwieldy, hard to repeat)

### G3. Line-Level Template
**FOR A 5-SYLLABLE HOOK:**
> [ Possessive ] + [ Body Part/Natural Image ] + [ Verb ]
> तेरी आँखों में रह जाऊँ = 7 syllables (works)

**FOR AN AA COUPLET:**
> Line 1: [ Image ] + [ Sensation ] + [ ending sound "a/aa" ]
> Line 2: [ Same image ] + [ Absence ] + [ rhyming ending ]
> तेरी ज़ुल्फ़ों में उंगलियाँ / फ़िर तेरी जान से जीने लग
