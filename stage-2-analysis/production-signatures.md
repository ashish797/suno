# 🎹 STAGE 2 — PRODUCTION SIGNATURES

*What the instrumentals sound like — BPM, keys, textures, and the sonic fingerprints of Hindi indie.*

---

## 🎛️ BPM (TEMPO) RANGES

### The BPM Map by Song Type:

| Song Type | BPM Range | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Heartbreak / Melancholic** | 70 - 85 | Slow enough to let the emotion breathe |
| **Lo-fi / Late-night** | 70 - 80 | Bedroom, intimate, unhurried |
| **Self-worth / Anthemic** | 90 - 105 | Groove + confidence, still emotional |
| **Nostalgic / Rain** | 75 - 90 | Gentle, builds without urgency |
| **Party / Freedom** | 110 - 130 | Drives forward, makes you move |
| **Travel / Road trip** | 100 - 120 | Rhythmic momentum |
| **Romantic (upbeat)** | 90 - 110 | Warm but energetic |
| **Monsoon romance** | 75 - 88 | Slow burn, rain-soaked |

### The 75 BPM Sweet Spot:
For lo-fi emotional Hindi indie, **75 BPM** is the single most reliable tempo. It matches a resting human heartbeat, feels intimate, and gives Suno enough room to generate clean melodies. If in doubt, start at 75 BPM.

## 🎹 KEYS (MUSICAL KEY)

### Common keys in Hindi Indie:

**C Major / A Minor** — Most common. Neutral, warm, easy to sing.
- Works for: heartbreak, self-worth, lo-fi

**G Major / E Minor** — Slightly brighter. Happy-sad.
- Works for: nostalgia, romantic

**D Major / B Minor** — Open, expansive.
- Works for: anthems, self-worth declarations

**A Major / F# Minor** — Uplifting, aspirational.
- Works for: party, freedom

**E Major / C# Minor** — Cinematic, warm.
- Works for: rain songs, emotional peaks

### Key guidance in prompts:
Just specify the key if you have a preference:
- `"C major"` — lets Suno tune the melody correctly
- `"dark minor key"` — for heartbreak
- `"bright major"` — for party/freedom

## 🎸 CORE INSTRUMENTATION

### The Hindi Indie Sound — Layer by Layer:

**Foundation Layer (always present):**
- Acoustic guitar — fingerpicking or strummed
- Light percussion — not heavy drums, usually minimal

**Mid Layer (adds texture):**
- Piano / keys
- Electric guitar (clean, not distorted)
- Subtle synth pad

**Top Layer (defines the vibe):**
- Vocal (the most important element)
- Ambient textures (rain, vinyl crackle, city sounds)
- Sparse additional layers

## 🎨 PRODUCTION TEXTURES BY SONG TYPE

### Lo-Fi / Late Night / Heartbreak:
```
Core: Acoustic guitar (fingerpicking), soft piano
Rhythm: Minimal, light finger snaps or soft kick
Texture: Vinyl crackle, rain sounds, low-fi warmth
Bass: Subtle, warm
Vocal: Breathy, close-mic, intimate
```
**Suno prompt keywords:** `"lo-fi, vinyl crackle, rain texture, intimate bedroom production, 75 BPM"`

### Party / Freedom / Travel:
```
Core: Acoustic guitar (strummed), synth
Rhythm: Full drums, driving snare
Texture: Bright, open, euphoric
Bass: Present, groovy
Vocal: Energetic, forward
```
**Suno prompt keywords:** `"upbeat acoustic guitar and synth, full drums, celebratory, 120 BPM"`

### Self-Worth / Anthemic:
```
Core: Acoustic guitar + electric guitar (clean)
Rhythm: Moderate, steady groove
Texture: Warm, open, anthemic build
Bass: Present
Vocal: Strong, confident, slightly breathy
Chorus: Full instrumentation build
```
**Suno prompt keywords:** `"warm acoustic and electric guitar, steady drums, anthemic chorus, 100 BPM"`

### Monsoon / Nostalgic Romance:
```
Core: Piano, acoustic guitar
Rhythm: Slow, gentle
Texture: Rain sounds, reverb, warm
Bass: Subtle
Vocal: Soft, intimate
Bridge: Optional orchestral swell
```
**Suno prompt keywords:** `"soft piano and acoustic guitar, rain texture, nostalgic, warm reverb, 80 BPM"`

### Lo-Fi Study / Chill:
```
Core: Lo-fi hip hop keys, guitar
Rhythm: Minimal, head-nod groove
Texture: Vinyl crackle, rain, cassette warmth
Bass: Present, rounded
Vocal: Quiet, almost spoken
```
**Suno prompt keywords:** `"lo-fi hip hop, vinyl crackle, rain, cassette warmth, soft piano, 75 BPM"`

## ⚠️ PRODUCTION MISTAKES TO AVOID

### ❌ Bollywood orchestration
Don't use: `"orchestral strings, dhol drums, filmi arrangement"`
Suno defaults to old Bollywood if you use these terms.
**Fix:** `"modern indie pop, clean production, not filmi"`

### ❌ Overproduction
Don't use: `"full band, layered synths, multiple percussion elements"`
Too much going on drowns the emotional intimacy.
**Fix:** `"minimal production, focus on vocals and guitar"`

### ❌ Auto-Tune overuse
Don't use: `"heavily processed vocals, strong auto-tune"`
Listeners want authenticity.
**Fix:** `"natural vocals, no auto-tune, breathy delivery"`

### ❌ English-only instrumentation
Don't use: `"trap drums, EDM drops, Western pop production only"`
Loses the Hindi indie identity.
**Fix:** `"Hindi indie sound, acoustic foundation, modern but desi"`

## 🏗️ PRODUCTION BUILD ARC

The song's production should breathe — it shouldn't all hit at once.

**Standard build:**
```
[Intro] — Bare — just guitar or ambient texture
[Verse 1] — Minimal — guitar + vocals, light percussion enters mid-verse
[Pre-chorus] — Build — adds piano or synth pad
[Chorus] — Full — all instruments in
[Verse 2] — Slight reduction — pulls back slightly from chorus
[Chorus 2] — Full again
[Bridge] — Different texture — maybe just piano + vocals
[Chorus 3] — Full + maybe one extra layer (backing vocals, extra reverb)
[Outro] — Fade — production drops out section by section
```

**The rule:** Never stay at maximum intensity. If everything is "on" all the time, nothing lands.
