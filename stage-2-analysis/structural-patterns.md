# 🏗️ STAGE 2 — STRUCTURAL PATTERNS

*How Hindi indie songs are actually built — the geometry of a viral track.*

---

## 📏 THE ANATOMY OF A VIRAL HINDI INDIE SONG

### Standard Structure (2:15 - 2:45 runtime)

```
[INTRO — 5-15 sec]
Sets mood, minimal. Sometimes just the hook melody with no lyrics.
No lyrics OR 1-2 whispered lines max.

[VERSE 1 — 8 lines, ~20-25 sec]
Setup. Establishes the emotional world.
Who is the narrator? What's the situation?
Builds toward the first chorus.

[CHORUS 1 — 4-8 lines, ~20-25 sec] ← THE HOOK
The main hook lands here. MUST land by 40 seconds total.
4 lines, repeated chorus structure.
The emotional peak of the song.

[VERSE 2 — 8 lines, ~20 sec]
Payoff. Deepens the story.
Takes the emotion somewhere new.
Builds to the final chorus.

[CHORUS 2 — 4-8 lines]
Repeat. Re-anchor the hook.
Often same as Chorus 1 with slight variation.

[BRIDGE — 4 lines, optional but common]
Emotional twist. Different melodic feel.
Often the "spiral" moment — goes somewhere unexpected.
Not every song needs it, but viral songs often have one.

[CHORUS 3 / OUTRO — 4-8 lines + fade or tag]
Final anchor. Either full stop or fade.
If there's a tag (artist credit), it goes here.

Total runtime: 2:15 - 2:45
```

---

## ⏱️ THE 40-SECOND RULE

**The hook must be clearly established within 40 seconds of the song starting.**

Why: On Reels/Shorts, if the hook hasn't hit by the time someone scrolls past, the song is dead. On Spotify, the skip rate in the first 30 seconds directly impacts algorithmic distribution.

### Hook can appear in:
- **Last line of Verse 1** (most common) — set up, then hook lands
- **First line of Chorus** — chorus hits, then verse continues
- **Pre-chorus** (if you have one) — short buildup section

### The hook placement patterns that work:

**Pattern A: Verse 1 ends → Chorus lands (classic)**
```
Verse 1 (8 lines) → Chorus (hook hits) → Verse 2 → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus
```
✅ Most common, reliable

**Pattern B: Chorus hits first (hook-forward)**
```
Chorus (hook hits first) → Verse 1 (story) → Chorus → Verse 2 → Chorus → Outro
```
✅ Maximum hook impact, risky if lyrics feel hollow

**Pattern C: Verse 1 = Chorus (short song)**
```
Verse/Chorus combo → Verse/Chorus → Bridge → Verse/Chorus
```
✅ For short songs under 2:00, high intensity

---

## 📐 THE 8-LINE VERSE RULE

**Standard verse length: 8 lines**

Why 8? It's enough to set up a story, short enough to not lose attention. Verses longer than 12 lines start feeling like the song is dragging.

### Verse 1 structure (setup):
```
Lines 1-2: Establish scene (time, place, emotional state)
Lines 3-4: Introduce the subject (person, feeling, situation)
Lines 5-6: Deepen the emotion (what it means, how it feels)
Lines 7-8: Set up the chorus (build toward the peak)
```

### Verse 2 structure (payoff):
```
Lines 1-2: Acknowledge the chorus hit ("you know that feeling...")
Lines 3-5: Take it somewhere deeper (what happened next, what it revealed)
Lines 6-7: Emotional escalation
Line 8: Bridge to final chorus
```

---

## 🎹 THE PRE-CHORUS (Optional but powerful)

**Pre-chorus = the "climb" before the peak**

It's a short section (2-4 lines) between Verse and Chorus that creates tension before the hook lands.

### Example structure:
```
[Verse 1 - 8 lines]
[Pre-chorus - 3 lines] ← tension builds
[Chorus - 4 lines] ← hook lands
```

### When to use a pre-chorus:
- When you want maximum emotional impact at the hook
- When the song is 2:30+ and needs that build
- For anthemic, self-worth songs (the climb amplifies the declaration)

### When to skip the pre-chorus:
- When the song is short (<2:15)
- When the hook is strong enough to carry immediately
- For late-night/intimate songs (less structure, more feeling)

---

## 🏔️ THE CHORUS ARCHITECTURE

### Standard Hindi Indie Chorus: 4 lines

The chorus is the anchor. Everything in the song exists to land the chorus.

**Line 1:** The hook line (the quotable, the one that stops scrolling)
**Line 2:** Supports / elaborates on the hook
**Line 3:** Emotional peak or twist
**Line 4:** Either completes the thought or leaves a thread

### The two chorus types:

**Type A: Declaration** (I am / I feel / I want)
```
Tera saath tha, dil nahi tha
Ab jaana hai, wapas nahi
```
Direct statement. Powerful. Works for independence anthems.

**Type B: Question/Dialogue** (tu kya / kyun / kahan)
```
Tera intezaar tha, tu kahan hai?
Dil ka poocha, koi javab nahi
```
Rhetorical question. Works for heartbreak, situationship.

### Chorus repeat rules:
- **Minimum 2 full choruses** (Verse 1 → Chorus → Verse 2 → Chorus)
- **Maximum 3 full choruses** (unless it's a very short chorus)
- If you have a bridge, put a chorus before AND after it

---

## 🌉 THE BRIDGE — The Emotional Twist

### Bridge = Where the song goes when you think you know where it's going

**Typical bridge (4 lines):**
- Different melodic contour (higher or lower register)
- Different rhythm feel
- Often uses a question or reflection
- Sometimes switches from "you" to "me" (narrator turns inward)

### Bridge placement:
```
Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus (final)
```

### When a bridge works best:
- Heartbreak songs (turns inward, reflects on self)
- Self-worth songs (declaration gets even stronger)
- Emotional escalation (the bridge takes it somewhere deeper)

### When to skip the bridge:
- Song is under 2:00
- The chorus is strong enough to carry without a twist
- The song has a call-and-response structure instead

---

## 🛫 THE OUTRO

### How songs end:

**Fade out** (most common) — Chorus repeats and fades
```
Chorus x2 → fade out over 10-15 sec
```
✅ Works for almost all emotional songs. Leaves them wanting more.

**Full stop** (less common) — Ends at full volume
```
Chorus final → hard stop
```
✅ Powerful for anthems. Commits fully.

**Tag** (increasingly common on AI platforms) — Brief mention of artist/song
```
Chorus final → "prod. by SUNO" or "this is [song name]"
```
⚠️ Risky — can feel cheap. Only use if Suno forces it. Better to fade.

**Spoken whisper outro** — One whispered line after the music
```
Instrumental plays out → whispered final line
```
✅ Intimate. Works for lo-fi / late-night songs.

## 📊 STRUCTURAL PATTERNS BY SONG TYPE

### Heartbreak song structure:
```
Intro (5 sec, minimal) → 
Verse 1 (8 lines, setup) → 
Chorus 1 (hook lands, emotional peak) →
Verse 2 (8 lines, deepening) →
Chorus 2 (repeat) →
Bridge (4 lines, turns inward) →
Chorus 3 (final) →
Fade out
```
**Length:** 2:30-2:45
**BPM:** 75-85
**Total lines:** ~40

### Self-worth anthem structure:
```
Intro (instrumental hook) →
Pre-chorus (build) →
Chorus 1 (declaration) →
Verse 1 (context) →
Chorus 2 →
Verse 2 (escalation) →
Chorus 3 →
Outro
```
**Length:** 2:30-2:45
**BPM:** 90-105
**Total lines:** ~44

### Party / Freedom song structure:
```
Intro (driven, 5 sec) →
Chorus first (hook lands immediately) →
Verse 1 (short, 4-6 lines) →
Chorus 2 →
Verse 2 (short) →
Chorus 3 →
Outro
```
**Length:** 2:15-2:30
**BPM:** 110-130
**Total lines:** ~30

### Lo-fi / Late-night structure:
```
Intro (ambient, 10 sec) →
Verse 1 (slow, 8 lines) →
Chorus 1 (quiet, intimate) →
Verse 2 →
Chorus 2 →
Outro (fade)
```
**Length:** 2:30-3:00
**BPM:** 70-80
**Total lines:** ~36

## 🚫 COMMON STRUCTURAL MISTAKES

1. **Too many verses** (more than 2) — kills momentum
2. **Hook lands too late** (after 60 seconds) — most listeners are gone
3. **No repetition of chorus** — hook needs to be reinforced
4. **Bridge before the second chorus** — disrupts flow
5. **Song over 3:00** — skip rate skyrockets
6. **Intro longer than 15 seconds** — impatient listeners leave
7. **Chorus too short** (2 lines) — doesn't land as hook
8. **No emotional arc** — every section feels the same
